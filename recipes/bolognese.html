Vegetarian Bolognese

Description

Who says being a vegetarian means you have to miss out on your favorite meals?

This dish is inspired by the classic Beef Bolognese, only without the meat. The taste is derived from the assortment of seasonings, spices, herbs, and sauces that add a twist and boost flavor, mimicking the savory taste of beef bolognese without the sacrifice of a cow.

This dish can be mixed & matched by playing around with the style of pasta, type of sauce, and variety of herbs, seasonings, and spices used. Find the perfect match for your taste buds or occasion! A family favorite that can be enjoyed timelessly.

Ingredients

  • Pasta of choice

    -mix & match different styles of pasta, or use themed pasta for some festivity fun

  • Sauce

    -Arrabiata sauce is preferable, but can also use marinara, tomato, or vodka sauce (as long as it is vegetarian)

  • Nutritional yeast (optional)
  • Meatless beef crumbles

    -I prefer Impossible Beef, but use your brand of choice, preferably crumbles

  • Herbs, spices, & seasonings

    -Vary spice level on tolerance. You can play around with what you add. I typically add Italian herbs, beef seasoning, Adobo, paprika, ginger, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, chili flakes, peppercorn, sea or regular salt

  • Oil (olive, castor, sesame, etc)

Steps

  1. Gather your ingredients, making sure to thaw frozen meatless meat if necessary
  2. Fill a large pot with water, sprinkle some salt, and bring to a boil
  3. In a large skillet (preferably cast-iron), drizzle some oil and place on low-to-medium heat
  4. Add meatless beef crumbles to skillet and break up with wooden spoon, ensuring even distribution over heat
  5. Loosely place a lid over the skillet and allow the crumbles to slowly cook (depending on brand, color should become darker as it cooks)
  6. Once water is boiling, add pasta of choice, making sure to stir often to avoid sticking together
  7. When meatless crumbles are browning, add spices and stir evenly into the mixture
  8. Add sauce of choice into the skillet with "meat" and mix thoroughly
  9. Add more seasonings or garnishing into the sauce mixture as needed
  10. Once pasta is at right texture (I prefer mine al dante), drain water and place back into large pot
  11. Add sauce and "meat" mixture to pasta and mix together, ensuring even distribution of sauce over pasta
  12. Place lid over pot and allow to sit for a few minutes to marinate in flavors
  13. Garnish as needed (optional)
  14. Voila! Enjoy with a dash of Nutritional Yeast or some bread
